1845 Bengal

James Esdaile and Hypnotism

What are the hidden powers of the mind?

Dr. Elliotson’s Zoist receives news that a British surgeon in Bengal, James Esdaile, has performed dozens of pain-free operations with mesmerised patients.

Esdaile observed local magicians

Inducing trance-like states, and presented himself as a fellow initiate into yogic powers of mind. He privately believed they were mumbo-jumbo, and had learned mesmerism from an English pamphlet.

His first patient

was a prisoner with a huge scrotal swelling which Esdaile removed painlessly in front or reliable witnesses: doctors, magistrates and the local governor.

News reports spread across India, and eventually… to Britain.

But what was the explanation?

The Lancet had already debunked mesmerism, yet the phenomenon was demonstrably real. Had Esdaile tapped into a mysterious ‘vital energy’?

The Scottish surgeon James Braid

has a radical new theory. He investigates the practices of Indian yogis and fakirs, particularly their trance states or mental ‘hibernation’, in which some can be buried alive for days.
